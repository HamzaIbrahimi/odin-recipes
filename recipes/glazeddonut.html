Tasty Glazed Donut

That Feeling when you Bite Into a Really Good Donut

There are few things in life as comforting and satisfying as biting into a warm, freshly glazed donut. The sweet, sticky glaze perfectly complements the fluffy, pillowy texture of the donut, creating a heavenly balance of flavors and textures. Whether you're looking for a breakfast treat, an afternoon snack, or a midnight indulgence, a glazed donut is always the perfect choice. With its irresistible aroma and mouthwatering taste, this classic pastry has earned its place as a beloved favorite of donut lovers everywhere. So, get ready to experience the ultimate donut bliss with this delicious glazed donut recipe!

Below, I share with you my secret recipe!

You Will Need the Following:

For the Donut

  • 1 ½ cups milk(360 mL), warm to the touch
  • 1 cup sugar(200 g)
  • ½ oz yeast(15 g)
  • 1 stick unsalted butter, soft
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 5 cups flour(625 g), sifted
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

For the Glaze

  • 3 cups powdered sugar(360 g)
  • 1 cup dry milk powder(70 g)
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • ½ cup water(120 mL), hot

And Here's How to Make Them

  1. In a medium bowl, add the milk, sugar, and yeast, stirring to combine.

  2. In a separate medium bowl, add the butter, eggs, and vanilla extract, stirring to combine.

  3. Add the milk mixture to the egg mixture and whisk to combine. Transfer to a large bowl and add the flour in thirds, stirring between each addition.

  4. Dump the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 5 minutes, until smooth.

  5. Gently poke the dough with a finger--the dough should bounce back up from the indentation.

  6. Place the dough in a large bowl, and lightly coat the dough with oil. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap.

  7. Let the dough rise in a warm place for an hour, or until doubled in size.

  8. Take the dough out and knead on a floured surface for 5 minutes until smooth.

  9. Place the dough in an oiled bowl and repeat with rise overnight in refrigerator.

  10. Roll out the dough on a floured surface until ¼ inch (6 mm) thick.

  11. Cut out the doughnuts and donut holes with a cookie cutter and piping tip.

  12. Melt enough shortening over medium heat to come up just 2 inches (5 cm) up on the side of a heavy frying pan. Heat the shortening to 375°F (190°C).

  13. Fry the donuts and donut holes for 45 seconds on each side, or until golden brown.

  14. In a bowl, combine the powdered sugar, dry milk powder, vanilla extract and hot water until smooth.

  15. Strain the glaze through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any lumps.

  16. Dunk the donuts in the glaze or drizzle the glaze on top.
